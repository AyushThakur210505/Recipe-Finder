Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Spaghetti alla Carbonara: A beloved Italian pasta dish, combining al dente spaghetti with a rich, creamy sauce made from eggs, cheese, and crispy pancetta or guanciale, topped with a sprinkle of black pepper for a burst of flavor.

Serve 4


40 mintues


169.3 calories / 100g

INGREDIENTS

  • 350g (12 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 50g (1.75 oz) Pecorino Romano cheese, grated (plus extra for serving)
  • 50g (1.75 oz) Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Olive oil (optional, for cooking)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

INSTRUCTIONS

Cook the spaghetti:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package instructions until al dente (usually around 9-10 minutes).
  3. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.

Prepare the sauce:

  1. While the spaghetti is cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale to the skillet and cook until crispy and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. If the pancetta/guanciale renders enough fat, you may not need to add any oil. If needed, add a drizzle of olive oil to the skillet.

Prepare the egg mixture:

  1. In a mixing bowl, beat the eggs.
  2. Add the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheeses to the beaten eggs, along with a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Mix well to combine.

Combine everything:

  1. Once the pancetta/guanciale is cooked, remove the skillet from heat.
  2. Add the drained spaghetti directly into the skillet with the pancetta/guanciale. Toss well to coat the spaghetti with the rendered fat.
  3. If the skillet is too hot, let it cool for a minute or two before adding the egg mixture to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  4. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the spaghetti and quickly toss everything together, ensuring the spaghetti is evenly coated with the creamy sauce. The heat from the spaghetti will cook the eggs, creating a creamy texture.
  5. If the sauce is too thick, gradually add some of the reserved pasta water until you reach your desired consistency.

Serve:

  1. Divide the spaghetti alla carbonara among serving plates.
  2. Sprinkle extra grated Pecorino Romano cheese and freshly ground black pepper on top.
  3. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired.
  4. Serve immediately while hot.

STORAGE

Serve Spaghetti carbonara immeditely, hot and tasty as they are. We do not recommend storing carbonara leftovers in the refrigerator or freezer for the following days. Make the carbonara and enjoy it freshly made!
